How to Compose Interesting Tweets on Twitter

Assembly time: 2-5 minutes
Engagement rating: A ton of retweets & likes

Are you a journalist with aspirations of expanding your horizons via social media? In this day and age, Twitter is a quintessential platform to a journalists success! You've came by the right place to learn how to write a "Thick Tweet". This skill will engage your audience which will lead to a bigger following.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Computer
  • Internet Access
  • Twitter
  • Hashtags & Pictures

  1. Log in to Twitter

    If you do not have a Twitter account, go ahead and make one now.

  2. Be original and post #hashtags

    This is the most important way to make your Twitter posts interesting. Nobody likes reading the same material over and over. If you want to grow and get followers, you need to post interesting and original material. #Hashtags will go a long way and help your profile gain exposure.

  3. Make use of your space

    With only 280 characters available to write, be sure to take advantage of the space. Too many people post a few words at the time. The things you say may be just as funny out of context since you do not have much space to explain a whole story, but be sure to use the space you DO have available to you!

  4. Be relevant

    Believe it or not, simple observations are a way to get people to relate to you. You want your followers to read your post and laugh out loud because it is a relatable experience that brings back a memory of an experience they had.

  5. Post image tweets and be active on Twitter

    Posting image tweets is a great way to differentiate your tweets from ones that do not have picures. Incorperating an image to your tweet can help you gain attention to your profile. We all know people who join social networking sites and post just once a month. This is not going to make you popular with anyone. As a matter of fact, not being active is the number 1 way to lose followers.
